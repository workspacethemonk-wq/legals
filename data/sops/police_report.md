# SOP: Filing a Police Report (FIR/GD) in India

1. **FIR (First Information Report)**: For cognizable offenses (e.g., theft, assault, murder).
    - Go to the nearest police station.
    - Give a verbal or written statement.
    - Ensure the FIR is read back to you if given orally.
    - **Free Copy**: You are entitled to a free copy of the FIR.
2. **GD (General Diary)**: For non-cognizable offenses (e.g., lost documents, minor scuffles).
3. **Zero FIR**: If you are away from the jurisdiction where the crime occurred, you can file a 'Zero FIR' at any police station. They must transfer it to the correct station later.

**Red Flags**:
- A police officer CANNOT refuse to file an FIR for a cognizable offense.
- If they refuse, you can send the complaint in writing to the Superintendent of Police (SP) or a Magistrate.
